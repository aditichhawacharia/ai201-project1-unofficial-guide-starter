It's one of the most competitive degrees and majors at the university, with essentially a standalone department/school within a school, in this case in Natural Sciences. It has two different honors programs, has the Friends of Computer Science program (connecting students to jobs, internships and opportunities in big tech — a huge benefit to students), and is highly ranked across all of its specialties. The DoD itself has involvement at hackathon events at the university pretty regularly. Some of the most well known and successful companies across the globe (IBM, Dell, Microsoft, AlienWare, Meta, Google) have either invested in the program or constantly pull students into their atmosphere due to the success of the program.

tactman | 2 years ago | Upvotes: 0 (reply to OP)
TAMU and UTD are good options in TX. Realistically, a lot of the same content is taught all over the USA. Because so many people apply to UT, the students in the classes are generally better and more capable than other places, so the teaching level is more rigorous. But if you go to other CS programs, it's not like you will miss out on knowledge. The effort you put into learning and your side projects has a bigger impact than who is teaching or which university you attended. In the workplace, you will find people from universities you have not heard of working alongside those from highly competitive universities. There is certainly prestige in graduating from a competitive university but for the most part, if you are really into your major and good at what you do, which university you went to won't hold you back.
