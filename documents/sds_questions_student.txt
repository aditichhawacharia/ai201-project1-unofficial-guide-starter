Dangerous-Basil1561 | 1 year ago | Upvotes: 2
SDS makes it easy to double major, a lot of built in room. I recommend to start your second major early (even if you don't end up doing it, it'll count for SDS breadth) and talk to the advisors of the major you want to add. You'll need to apply, but you should be able to do basic courses before that. I'm graduating in 4 years with SDS and Geology, but I had a decent amount of credits and I took summer courses last year.
Freshman SDS courses are pretty easy, so far my sophomore ones are a lot harder but doable.
You don't really need to have coding experience. The fall freshman course you'll take teaches you how to code in R. You'll also have to take Python courses. They are for all levels of coding, but I might recommend lightly brushing up on Python beforehand.
