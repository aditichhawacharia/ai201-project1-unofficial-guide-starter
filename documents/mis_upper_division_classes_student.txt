arioid | 6 years ago | Upvotes: 4
It varies a lot. For electives like 373, there are topics that go into code and topics that do deep conceptual discussions with tons of industry professionals. 374 is a consulting experience where you get paired with a client and work on a project for them, so that can be extremely technical but will vary based on the client you get — time commitment is higher than average for a MIS class, but again will vary based on your client. 375 is more case-driven discussions plus a project.
Ultimately, lots of opportunities for technical experiences. If you're looking for more, I'd recommend adding the Elements of Computing Certificate and taking some additional classes from there. For grade distribution information, look at Catalyst's Distribution data or the data available in the UT Registration Plus Extension on the course catalog.
